\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% We presented a measurement study of three remote shell protocols, SSHv2, Mosh, and SSH3, under terminal workloads that reflect how interactive AI agents drive a remote shell. The results show that no single protocol is best across all workloads. The right choice depends on which interaction pattern dominates. SSH3 establishes sessions fastest and gives the lowest latency for short command loops while preserving near-complete output. Mosh achieves the lowest keystroke latency by a wide margin and suits latency-sensitive interactive editing, but its screen-synchronization design delivers only 2-6\% of large command outputs. SSHv2 is matched or improved upon by SSH3 in nearly every scenario, with complete output delivery its main remaining strength.
