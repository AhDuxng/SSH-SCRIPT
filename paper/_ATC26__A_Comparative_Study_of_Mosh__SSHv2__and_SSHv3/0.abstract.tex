\begin{abstract}
%The development of intelligent devices using wireless networking has spurred research on new protocols, for multipath transport, such as MPTCP and MPQUIC. For these protocols, scheduling is one of the critical processes to ensure optimal transmission performance. Although many studies have proposed multipath scheduling algorithms, the current approaches still have limitations in network conditions with multi-clients and heterogeneous paths, as in the real environment. In this paper, we propose a Centralized Learning-based MPQUIC Scheduler in Heterogeneous Wireless Networks named CeLeM, which efficiently schedules multipath communication in heterogeneous wireless networks. By leveraging the self-learning ability of reinforcement learning, an MPQUIC device using CeLeM can instantly observe environmental changes and deploy appropriate path selection for each packet sent to optimize data transmission time. Our evaluation results show that CeLeM improves the data transmission time by 7\%-16\% in simulation deployment compared to the state-of-the-art schedulers.

% In the context of the evolving 5G, intelligent mobile devices are capable of accessing many heterogeneous wireless networks (e.g., Wi-Fi and 5G). To optimize the use of these network resources, multipath transport protocols like MPTCP and MPQUIC offer substantial potential. In such protocols, scheduling is crucial in ensuring optimize multipath data transmission. Despite numerous proposed multipath scheduling solutions, their performance remains limited in the context of the server having to handle resource requests from multiple clients concurrently. In this paper, we propose a novel centralized learning-based MPQUIC scheduler to optimize data transmission throughout the network (CeLeM), in order to address these challenges.
% By harnessing the self-learning capabilities of reinforcement learning, CeLeM adeptly adapts to shifting network characteristics in dynamic environments to have an optimal decision-making policy. Furthermore, it adopts a central controller to consider the state of the network based on the change in the network characteristics over all subflows. Our evaluation results show that CeLeM improves the global download time by 7\%-16\% in simulation deployment compared to the state-of-the-art schedulers.
% With the evolution of 5G, intelligent mobile devices can now access multiple heterogeneous wireless networks (e.g., Wi-Fi and 5G) simultaneously. 
% In such a context, multipath transport protocols, such as MPTCP and MPQUIC, which enable clients to simultaneously transmit data over multiple interfaces, arise as potential solutions to maximize the utilization of all available networks. A multipath transport protocol relies heavily on the path scheduler, which determines which path to use during each timeslot. 
% Despite numerous efforts devoted to the design of schedulers, all existing approaches only consider a single-client context. These single-client-oriented schedulers, when applied to multiple client scenarios (which is likely to occur in practice), will suffer from a so-called selfish scheduling phenomenon, in which decisions made by each scheduler only consider the context of the client it is responsible for, resulting in conflicts of interest between schedulers.
% In this work, we are the first to consider the existence of multiple clients when dealing with the scheduling problem for multipath transport protocols. We especially focus on MPQUIC and propose MuLeS, a learning-based scheduler for MPQUIC in the multi-client scenario. Our evaluation results show that MuLeS outperforms contemporary schedulers in terms of various metrics, including download time, good-put, and loss rate. Notably, MuLeS reduces the average download time by 7\%-16\% compared to the state-of-the-art schedulers.




\end{abstract}
